About the Project

Chernobium is a project to remind and educate the public about what happened in Chernobyl and what impact it has our lives today.

About Chernobyl

Chernobyl is ghost town, named after the species of wormwood. It is one of the tourist attractions in the Ukraine. Before the disaster it was a home to more than 116,000 people. On April 26, 1986 the nuclear reactor number 4 went through performance testing. Due to the failures in the design, and the human factor the reactor overheated and exploded. Releasing tons of radioactive isotopes into the atmosphere. Now reactor number 4 is the most radioactive place on the planet.
